Insulin   

IV Insulin

   

Single Strength: 0.1 Units/mL: Add 5 units Actrapid® to 50mls Dextrose 5%
Double Strength: 0.2 Units/mL: Add 10 units Actrapid® to 50mls Dextrose 5%

Typical insulin requirements for the management of neonatal hyperglycaemia are 0.05 – 0.12 units/kg/hour; in other words a 1000g infant is likely to require from 0.2 – 0.6 mL/hour of the "double strength" solution.

Start with a modest insulin dose eg ~ 0.05 units/kg/hour and adjust based on the blood sugar trends.

A formal "sliding scale" is usually less effective than individualised dosing decisions made after each blood sugar measurement is obtained.

A falling (though still high) blood sugar level does not always require an immediate drop in the insulin rate. Slowing the infusion too early often results in blood sugar "rebound".

Insulin is now to be counted in the daily fluid requirement.

Lipids

Clinoleic 20% Lipid

Clinoleic is the "standard" lipid emulsion and as the sole lipid emulsion is given at up to 3 g/ kg/ day.

Because vitamins are added to the lipid syringe the effective lipid concentration of clinoleic is approximately 14%.

Therefore, clinoleic 1 g/kg/day = 0.3 mL/kg/hour

Omegaven 10% Lipid

Omegaven is "fish lipid" and is used for infants at higher risk of TPN-related liver disease. It is used in conjunction with clinoleic with each given at 1 g/ kg/ day.

Because vitamins are added to the lipid syringe the effective lipid concentration of omegaven is approximately 8.8%.

Therefore, Omegaven 1 g/kg/day = 0.5 mL/kg/hour

Monitoring

Maintain serum triglyceride below 2.8 mmol/L (~250 mg/dL)
